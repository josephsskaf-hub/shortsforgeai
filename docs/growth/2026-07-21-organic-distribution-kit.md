### Claims that are safe to publish

- A topic or pasted script can become a finished vertical video with script, AI voiceover, scene-matched footage, captions and MP4 output.
- The normal product format is 9:16 for YouTube Shorts, TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- A source or long-form video is not required for the topic-to-video workflow.
- Fast Mode uses matched stock footage and is **typically** ready in about 60 seconds; never turn that into a guaranteed render time.
- New accounts can create, watch, download and share up to three watermarked Fast videos every 24 hours without a card.
- Paid plans provide clean exports and can access available engines when the account has enough credits.
- “Use my script as is,” Character Lock, AI Presenter, My Videos history and per-scene B-roll planning exist in the product.
- Failed renders are automatically eligible for credit refund through the product's refund path; avoid promising an exact refund time in directory copy.

### Claims that must not appear

- Guaranteed virality, views, revenue, RPM, retention or subscriber growth.
- Invented creator/customer counts, ratings, awards, reviews or performance benchmarks.
- “Unlimited” videos, “free credits,” “no watermark” for free users, or “first video free.”
- A guaranteed identical face, voice or style in every output. Describe Character Lock and AI Presenter as tools for consistency, not a perfect-result guarantee.
- “60 seconds” without “typically” or “about.” Premium AI engines commonly take longer.
- Team collaboration, direct social publishing, scheduling, moderation controls, an API or integrations unless the live product is re-audited and proves the specific claim.

- Topic-to-video and script-to-video workflows.

### Differentiation without competitor overclaims

1. **Idea-first, not source-video-dependent:** a user can begin with one topic rather than a long video that must already exist.
2. **Whole Short in one workflow:** scripting, narration, scene planning, footage, captions, rendering and MP4 export are connected.

4. **Multiple visual approaches:** matched stock footage for Fast Mode and generated-scene engines for paid credit balances.
5. **Recurring-show tools:** Character Lock and AI Presenter support a more consistent on-screen identity without promising perfect identity preservation.
6. **Try-before-subscription path:** the free allowance produces downloadable, shareable watermarked output without a card.

## 10. Measurement through an active recurring subscription

The campaign name in section 3 is the join key. Do not report a directory as successful from a submission, approval, click, signup or expired checkout alone.

### Evidence chain

1. **Discovery:** public listing/GPT URL exists and is accessible while logged out.
2. **Qualified visit:** `landing_session_started` attributed to the exact channel source, medium and campaign.
3. **High-intent action:** `organic_topic_submitted` on the destination landing.
4. **Signup:** external profile stores `signup_utm_source`, `signup_utm_medium` and `signup_utm_campaign` for that channel.
5. **Activation:** the attributed signup creates a `videos.status = 'completed'` record.
6. **Commercial intent:** identifiable `pricing_view`, `checkout_started` and a recurring Stripe Checkout Session.
7. **Revenue:** a new external Stripe subscription has status `active` or `trialing`. This is the success event. `payment_success` is supporting telemetry; Stripe subscription state is authoritative.

### Reporting command

Run from the repository root:

```powershell
npm.cmd run growth:measure -- --days=1
npm.cmd run growth:measure -- --days=7
npm.cmd run growth:measure -- --days=14
```

Read these report sections:

- `acquisition.signupCampaigns`
- `acquisition.recurringStripeSessionsByCampaign`
- `acquisition.newSubscriptionsByCampaign`
- `funnel.newActiveOrTrialingSubscriptions`

The script excludes known internal/test accounts and does not print customer emails or user IDs.

### Initial gate per channel

Use **14 days or 30 qualified sessions, whichever comes first**:

- Signup rate target: at least 15% of qualified sessions.
- First completed video target: at least 10% of qualified sessions.
- Purchase-intent target: at least one recurring Stripe Checkout Session.
- Commercial success: at least one new external `active` or `trialing` recurring subscription attributed to the channel.

Interpretation rules:

- Zero qualified sessions after a verified public listing means a discovery/distribution problem, not a landing-page conversion failure.
- Thirty sessions with fewer than three topic submissions means listing-to-landing promise mismatch; revise title/category/copy before adding another directory.
- Attributed signups without a completed video mean an activation problem.
- Completed videos without pricing views mean the post-value upgrade path is the likely break.
- Recurring Stripe Sessions that remain open or expire without payment are intent evidence only; diagnose checkout trust, payment methods and plan fit.
- Count a payer only once, by the source/medium/campaign stored on the matched external profile and a Stripe subscription status of `active` or `trialing`.
- For GPT Store, also capture the GPT's available usage/conversation analytics so zero site sessions can be separated into “no Store discovery” versus “GPT used but CTA ignored.”
