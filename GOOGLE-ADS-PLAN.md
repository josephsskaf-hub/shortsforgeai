## 1. Campaign Type & Objective

**Primary: Google Search Campaign**
**Secondary: None at this budget** (Performance Max and YouTube Ads require more data and budget runway to optimize effectively — save for week 2)

**Why Search?**
At R$100 over two days (~R$50/day), Search is the only format that lets you target people *actively searching* for your exact problem — "how to make YouTube Shorts fast" or "AI video creator." You capture existing demand with zero waste on passive audiences. Performance Max needs conversion data to optimize; YouTube pre-roll burns budget on unqualified views. Search gives you the tightest control at this budget level.

**Campaign Objective:** Website conversions (sign-ups)
Set up Google Ads conversion tracking on the registration/sign-up confirmation page before launch. Without this, you're flying blind.

## 5. Bidding Strategy

**Day 1:** Use **Maximize Clicks** with a max CPC bid cap of **R$3.50**
- Why: You have zero conversion data. Maximize Conversions with no history will overspend or underspend erratically.
- The R$3.50 cap prevents runaway spend on competitive terms while still being competitive in US/UK English markets. If you see almost no impressions, raise to R$5.00.
- Expected range: CPC of R$1.50–R$3.50 in these markets for mid-competition keywords.

**Day 2:** If you received 3+ conversions on Day 1, switch to **Maximize Conversions** (no target CPA yet). If fewer than 3 conversions, stay on Maximize Clicks but tighten to your best-performing keywords only.

**Do not use Target CPA yet** — it needs 30+ conversions in 30 days to work properly.

## 8. KPIs to Track

At R$100 over 2 days, you won't have statistically significant data for most metrics — but these are your north stars:

| KPI | What You're Looking For | Benchmark to Aim For |
|-----|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Impressions** | Are ads showing at all? | >500/day |
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | Is ad copy resonating? | >3% is good for Search |
| **CPC (Cost Per Click)** | Efficiency of spend | R$1.50–R$4.00 target |
| **Conversions (Sign-ups)** | Primary goal | Even 2–5 is meaningful at this budget |
| **Cost Per Conversion** | Efficiency benchmark | Aim for <R$25 (=1 Basic plan sub covers cost) |
| **Search Impression Share** | How often ads show vs. possible | >30% is healthy |
| **Quality Score** | Ad relevance (check keyword-level) | Aim for 5+ out of 10 |

**Access these in:** Google Ads → Campaigns → Columns (customize to add Conversions, Cost/Conv, CTR)

## 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid at This Budget Size

**1. Using Broad Match keywords**
Broad Match at R$50/day in English-speaking markets = your budget is gone by 10am on irrelevant traffic. Stick to Exact and Phrase only.

**2. Running too many campaigns simultaneously**
One campaign, one ad group (or max two tightly themed groups). Splitting R$50 across 3 campaigns means none of them has enough data to optimize.

**3. Not adding negative keywords before launch**
"Free YouTube Shorts maker," "YouTube Shorts tutorial," "how to download Shorts" — these will eat your budget. Build your negative list before you go live.

**4. Changing settings daily**
Google's algorithm needs 24–48 hours to stabilize after any change. Don't panic-edit bids every 2 hours. One review per day is enough.

**5. Targeting too many countries at once**
US CPCs are high. UK is slightly cheaper. Canada and Australia are moderate. If budget paces out by noon, pause Canada and Australia and focus on UK only (lower CPCs, still English-speaking, strong YouTube culture).

**6. Forgetting conversion tracking**
If the sign-up conversion tag isn't firing correctly, Google Ads reports zero conversions and you have no data to optimize on. Test the tag with Google Tag Assistant before launching.

**7. Sending traffic to the homepage with no clear CTA**
If the homepage has 6 different things to click, your conversion rate dies. The page should have one job: get the sign-up.

**8. Expecting scale at R$100**
This is a test budget. The real goal is to validate: Do people click? Do they sign up? What does a conversion cost? Use this data to build a proper monthly budget. If CPC comes in at R$2.50 and conversion rate at 5%, that's R$50/sign-up — useful intelligence for scaling.
